About Me

I'm an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at Carnegie Mellon University with a 4.0 GPA, passionate about building software that makes a difference. My journey into development started with curiosity about how systems work, and that curiosity continues to drive everything I create today.

Currently, I'm a developer at Scotty Labs, where I work on student-focused applications using Next.js, React, TypeScript, and PostgreSQL. I recently partnered with the Graduate Student Assembly to architect a full-stack housing review website, translating student feedback into technical specifications and user-focused design to promote housing transparency on campus.

I learn best by building complete projects from scratch. Whether it's developing a tower defense game in Python with Pygame, creating regression models in MATLAB to analyze housing trends, or building full-stack web applications, I believe in understanding every layer of the stack. I value clean architecture, thoughtful design, and code that's built to last.

Beyond academics, I spent years managing operations at my family's restaurant, where I learned the importance of efficiency, customer service, and giving back to the community through monthly food donations. These experiences taught me that good software, like good service, is about understanding people's needs and solving real problems.
